About

Oct 12, 2020

Current Position

I’m currently a Research Associate in the Integrative Biology department at the University of Texas at Austin, working with Dr. Lauren Ancel Meyers. Together, we run the UT COVID-19 Modeling Consortium, an interdisciplinary network of researchers and health professionals building models to detect, project, and combat COVID-19, where I’m currently serving as Associate Director.

My Background

I went to college at Rose-Hulman Insitute of Technology. After a short stint working towards a computer engineering degree, I switched to a biology, fell in love with research, and became fascinated with infectious diseases.

I came to UT Austin in 2013 as part of the Ecology, Evolution, and Behavior graduate program and completed my research advised by Dr. Lauren Ancel Meyers. My PhD focused on integrating statistics, computation, and epidemiology to understand pandemic emergence risk and response looking at emerging infectious diseases like influenza, Zika, and Ebola.

I finished in the Spring of 2018, and wanted to dip my toe into industry to get real-world data science experience. I was attracted to the tech startup scene in Austin, and was lucky to get the opportunity to work at two high-growth startups over a couple of years, though I missed the academic research environment almost immediately. As COVID-19 began its spread around the globe, it became clear that it was the right time to return to academics and help with the response and I rejoined the Meyers Lab to help launch the UT-Austin COVID-19 Modeling Consortium.
